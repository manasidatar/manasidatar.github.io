Shapes with Nonregular Surfaces

methods for robust correspondences

Isotropy invariant correspondences via relative distances

The use of geodesic distances to a-priori landmarks to optimize isometry invariant correspondences to address challenges posed by the inherent complexity (e.g. cortical surface shapes) and variability (e.g. cardiac chambers) evident in many biomedical shapes.

Improved correspondences via feature entropy

An efficient method incorporating geodesic distances and an intershape penalty based on surface normals for improved correspondences on highly nonregular surfaces.

Correspondences for open surfaces

The definition of surface boundaries as geometric primitives and formulation of interactions for minimal influence of constraints on shape model with the aim of computing correspondence positions on a set of open surfaces. [w/ J Cates]

Shape analysis for Cam femoroacetabular impingement (FAI): femur study

The use of statistical shape modeling to compare and quantify morphologic differences between control and FAI femurs characterized by a malformed femoral head that may lead to early hip osteoarthritis. [w/ MD Harris]

Shape analysis for hip dysplasia: acetabular cartilage study

The quantification and visualization of shape differences in acetabular cartilage between controls and dysplasia patients to give a preview of how form (i.e., shallow acetabulum) relates to function (e.g., altered cartilage contact pressures).

Shape analysis for multiple osteochondromas: mouse model study

A study of the 'steal phenomenon' caused by multiple osteochondromas in mouse models was performed by comparing bone lengths and volumes and correlating bone length deviations with metaphyseal volume deviations from normal (as a measure of osteochondroma volumetric growth). [w/ KB Jones]

Shape analysis for ischemia

A framework to study anatomic variability and perform hypothesis testing to quantify shape changes related to heart failure remodeling. [w/ Dr. R Winslow]

Shape change over time

methods for regression of correspondences

Linear shape regression

The incorporation of linear regression analysis to build shape models based on correspondence optimization and evaluation based on permutation testing, along with an application to obtain a normative model of early head shape development from clinical MRI data. [w/ J Cates]

Linear mixed-effects (LME) shape model

A method to characterize longitudinal shape changes in anatomy, combining point correspondences across shapes with statistical modeling of individual and population trends via the LME model. [w/ P Murlidharan, A Kumar]

Shape analysis for atrial fibrillation (AF): human heart study

A proof-of-concept study using the LME shape model to quantify left atrium (LA) shape change after successful radiofrequency (RF) ablation, with results uncovering subtle shape changes beyond simple volume differences and indicating further investigation of clinical hypotheses relating LA shape to AF. [w/ J Cates]

Shape analysis for atrial fibrillation (AF): canine heart study

An investigation of population trends in atrial shape change during AF in a canine model where the LME model consistently detects subtle atrial shape changes across groups in spite of variation in anatomy across individuals and can be leveraged for clinical assessment of AF progression. [w/ J Cates, J Jenson]

Machine learning methods

sample selection, image segmentation and then some...

Natural scene segmentation

The use of a two-stage hierarchical self-organizing map (HSOM) for segmentation of natural scene images, where sample selection based on homogeneity coupled with the information fusion mechanism (color and texture information), led to good segmentation results, validated on a variety of images.

Molecular pathology image segmentation

An intuitive approach for segmenting pathology slide images via the hierarchical self-organizing map (HSOM), introducing the use of unsupervised clustering using both color and texture features and the use of unsupervised color merging outside of the HSOM framework with an application to prostrate cancer staging.

Image orientation detection

An algorithm for automated image orientation detection, based on the supervised self-organizing map (SOM) that uses a compact feature vector and achieves decent accuracy with minimal training time and a small set of training images with ground truth orientations.

Multimodality image registration

methods to incorporate material properties, validation

Incorporating spatially varying degrees of freedom (DOF)

A technique that aligns tissue types based on spatially varying DOF for intrasubject deformable registration such that smoothness constraints applied to the deformation to accurately align soft tissue do not distort more rigid tissue types.

Anatomically guided registration

A simple anatomically guided approach for whole body image registration offering enhanced alignment of large coverage inter-scan studies with anatomy specific transformations to capture independent motions.

Correlating perfusion/diffusion with anatomy

A clinical study designed to quantify the performance of multimodality registration algorithms in registering functional (PET/DWI) images with corresponding anatomical images (MRI) using point correspondences.

Miscellaneous

statistics, GPGPU, vision, others...

Quantification and visualization of variation in tree space

A method for quantification and visualization of variation in datasets of trees by localization of subtrees displaying significant population differences, computed via hypothesis testing and sparse classification of subtree features.

GPU acceleration for particle systems

The implementation of an efficient parallel algorithm for sampling an arbitrary surface via dynamic distribution of points (aka particles) using GPGPU programming. [w/ B Petersen]

Gaze tracking and its relation to perception of symmetry

The implementation of a gaze detection and tracking system using learning based classification on a set of visual cues to test the hypothesis that such cues can be used to track the perception of symmetry and discontinuities therein. [w/ N Singh]
